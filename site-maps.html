Site Maps

What is it?

A sitemap is a file where you can list the web pages of your site to tell search engines about the organization of your site content.

What is it used for?

  • Allows webmasters to submit a master list of all of their sites pages to search engines for indexing.
  • Provide valuable metadata associated with the pages you list within it (metadata is information about a webpage, such as when the page was last updated, how often the page is changed, and the importance of the page relative to other URLs in the site).
  • To help the search engines to index all of the content present on the site and crawl your site more intelligently by, for example, notifying them when pages are added or removed.

A good sitemap should accomplish the following:

  • Your XML Sitemap should include the following tags:
    1. <lastmod> - notifies them of when a page last changed.
    2. <changefreq> - indicates how often the page is likely to change.
    3. <priority> - set a crawl priority to site pages based on their importance, relative to the rest of the site.
  • To have the most recent version of your URL’s at all times.
  • Each Sitemap must have no more than 50,000 URLs and must be no larger 10MB (after un-gzipping). There are options to compress Sitemap files using ‘gzip’ to reduce the amount of bandwidth required.
  • Specify all URLs using the same syntax.
